Help The Manager Help You

13 Dec 2019 in Blog

What duties do we have towards our managers to help them manage us?

We all praise or blame our bosses for our development throughout our career. We thank the manager when we get a promotion, attend trainings and conferences or get assigned to an interesting project. We also blame them when we do not get these things. And indeed, a manager can have a big influence on our career growth. However, we usually tend to forget that the manager is only part of the equation to be happy in our job and grow a satisfying career. The other part of the equation is us. Let’s help managers help us.

Managers are only part of our happiness equation. The other part is us.

To reach our destination, the first step is to identify where do we want to go. This is much easier said than done. We should identify what makes us happy on the long run, what do we enjoy most at work and what do we want to learn and explore. Then we should set clear short term goals that will push us towards our destination or vision. It is very unrealistic to expect a manager to find out what will make us happy and set goals for us that are inline with our vision. When our goals are set, we should articulate them to our manager and be very clear about it. Asking for clear guidance and advice from a manager who is usually more experienced is very important, although some people might be discouraged to ask for help. We should articulate our needs clearly; managers can’t read minds. After we set our goals and express them, we should expect from our manager to help us reach them, advocate for those goals and give us the opportunity to pursue them. However, doing so does not guarantee that the manager will be able to put us where we want to be. If we asked for a promotion and worked hard for it, the job of the manager is to advocate for our promotion but they cannot always guarantee it. There are many other factors that plays a role in decision making process for some of our demands that are not in the hands of our manager. It is important to put ourselves in the manager’s shoes before judging whether we have been treated fairly or not. This will also help us have more empathy towards the manager when we understand that they are humans, they share the same struggles with us and they will make mistakes.

We should articulate our needs clearly. Managers can’t read minds.

Another important factor for our career growth is feedback. In our career journey, we should expect to get feedback on regular basis from managers. If we are not getting feedback, then we should not wait but ask for it. When we do that, we expect to have some negative feedback that might feel hurtful or untrue. But with an open mind this is a great opportunity to learn and improve and become better. We should learn to embrace constructive feedback.

If we asked for specific trainings, projects, skills or promotions and the manager did not give us the opportunity to pursue them. If we are not getting any feedback even when we ask for it. If after applying all the above steps we did not get the expected response and support from the manager. Only then it is a clear sign that the manager will not help us grow in our career and reach our destination. In that case the best solution might be changing for a new manager.
